Bio

Hvem er jeg?

My name is Christian Nielsen Garcia, and I'm currently a PhD student at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). My research revolves around leveraging digital tools to foster a freer, more democratic, and—quite frankly—cooler society. Within the academic world, I navigate realms like Computational Social Science, Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW), Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and Data Studies. However, I prefer to describe my work differently: I spend my time exploring the intersection between the digital information structures we inhabit and the relationships between individuals and their collectives. Through this intersection, I investigate the co-production of technology and people, and I strive to imagine ways that machines and humans can collaborate to create free and awesome utopias.

I’m well aware of the idealistic tone in that description of my work, so let me ground it away from the "metaversical" ideals by giving you a sense of what I actually do. My work spans two broad categories:

Uno - Academic Inquiry and Expression

The first involves academic research and its dissemination. This, of course, includes traditional papers and articles, but it also extends to video essays, website-essays, online courses, and decentralized digital courses. Some of these projects play with the conventional assumptions about how researchers should share their work.

Due - Building Tools for Digital-Social Empowerment

The second category encompasses tools or creative/interactive works designed for users, with the goal of empowering them as the foundation for a freer digital society. These tools are often designed from the ground up, informed by the concepts and discussions that I also research—such as digital society, automation, decentralization, and the rhizomatic effects of these factors on our ability to realize digital dreams of a freer, cooler, and more badass society.
