On Computational Discourse Analyses in DAOs - Article for Flipside Governance

This study leverages advanced language modeling to analyze 59,000 forum posts from 15 decentralized autonomous organizations (DAOs), aiming to identify prevalent governance topics within these communities. Given the vast volume of discourse and the challenges of staying informed, this research highlights the value of computational discourse analysis in uncovering significant themes. Key findings reveal that DAO discussions predominantly focus on two broad categories: scalability issues and governance participation. Under scalability, topics include transaction fees, scaling solutions, and cross-chain interoperability. For governance participation, discussions center on delegation concerns, incentivization, and delegate compensation. This preliminary analysis underscores the importance of computational methods in distilling actionable insights from DAO discussions, offering a streamlined approach for stakeholders to engage with current governance debates and trends.
